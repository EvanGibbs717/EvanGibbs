Hello and welcome to the page!  

I am Evan Gibbs, and this site is here as:  
**(1)** a showcase of what I do   
**(2)** a little hobby and repository for things I care about.  

I completed my undergraduate in Mathematics at York College of Pennsylvania under my advisor Dr. Haiping Yuan and am currently transitioning into a Ph.D. in Mathematics beginning Fall 2026. (Very) broadly, I am interested in mathematical and theoretical ecology. If you want to learn more about this, you can check out my [Research and Academics](/EvanGibbs/research/) and [Research Projects](/EvanGibbs/researchproj/) tabs. Much of my interest in these topics comes from my rural background in Pennsylvania where I spent most of my time fishing, hiking, and appreciating nature. The outdoors hold a very special and dear place in my heart, and as such, I believe the conservation and betterment of our Earth is crucially important.   

At its core, mathematics can help us formalize and deeply understand the mechanisms we see in nature. In a more applied sense, mathematics can also assume a critical role in guiding environmental conservation strategies, as well as making locations more hospitable and enjoyable for humans. It can also be a strong motivator for influencing policy related to such topics. My dream is to combine these three domains of mathematics, nature, and people, so that I can develop generalizable mathematical frameworks that help ecologists do their job even better. I am still very much a baby in this field but am excited to keep learning!
